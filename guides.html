Guides

Below are self-help guides for achieving relaxation.

Grounding Technique

5-4-3-2-1 Method

This exercise uses your five senses to bring attention back to the present moment. It can help reduce anxiety by grounding you in your surroundings.

Look around and find:

  • 5 things you can see
  • 4 things you can touch
  • 3 things you can hear
  • 2 things you can smell
  • 1 thing you can taste

Breathing Techniques

Box Breathing

Slow, controlled breathing helps calm the nervous system and reduce stress. A simple pattern is called box breathing:

  1. Inhale for 4 seconds
  2. Hold for 4 seconds
  3. Exhale for 4 seconds
  4. Hold for 4 seconds

4-7-8 Breathing

This technique slows the heart rate and promotes a calming state by extending the exhale and holding the breath briefly.

  1. Inhale through your nose for 4 seconds
  2. Hold for 7 seconds
  3. Exhale through your mouth for 8 seconds
  4. Repeat 4–6 cycles

Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Progressive Muscle Relaxation reduces physical tension by tensing and relaxing muscle groups in sequence.

  • Move gradually from head to toe (or vice versa)
  • Tense a muscle group for 5–7 seconds
  • Release the tension and hold for 10–15 seconds
  • Continue until all of your muscles are relaxed
